PV Mapping for Urban India

Estimate rooftop solar potential with physically grounded methods

This platform helps planners and researchers quickly evaluate rooftop solar opportunities in dense urban regions. Instead of a black-box estimate, it shows how weather, shadows, and environmental penalties shape expected output.

Scroll to explore the data pipeline, then enter the interactive map to run AOI and per-building calculations.

Data Engine

Google Earth Engine powers the geospatial workflow

We use Google Earth Engine to combine ERA5 irradiance, Open Buildings footprints, and terrain context into a unified analysis workflow. This allows fast server-side geospatial computation while keeping interaction simple in the browser.

Methodology

Penalties and rooftop physics are explicitly modeled

  • Shadow penalty: insolation-weighted shadow frequency from building geometry and solar positions.
  • Urban heat penalty: derating from heat-island temperature effects.
  • Soiling penalty: retention factor derived from aerosol proxies.
  • Yield conversion: net irradiance ? energy using panel efficiency and performance ratio.

These assumptions are transparent so users can interpret and compare output quality across locations and time windows.
